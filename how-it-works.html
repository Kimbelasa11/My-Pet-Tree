How It Works

From identifying the right locations to nurturing mature trees, discover our process for building thriving urban forests.

Our Four-Step Process

1

Community Assessment

We start by listening to neighborhoods. What's the canopy coverage? What species thrive locally? What do residents want? Our team meets with community leaders and residents to understand needs.

2

Site Preparation

We identify optimal planting sites—streets, parks, and yards that will benefit most from tree coverage. We prepare soil, secure permissions, and coordinate logistics with local partners.

3

Tree Planting Event

Communities gather for a planting day. Local residents, volunteers, and our team plant trees together. It's an event that builds connection and investment in the neighborhood's green future.

4

Stewardship & Growth

Our work doesn't end at planting. We provide 3 years of care, support, and monitoring. Residents take over long-term stewardship, ensuring trees thrive for decades.

Why Neighborhoods Adopt

Cool Streets

Summer temperatures can drop by 2-5°C on streets with mature tree canopy, reducing energy costs and making neighborhoods more livable.

Health & Wellbeing

Green spaces improve mental health, reduce stress, and encourage outdoor activity. Trees make neighborhoods safer and more attractive places to live.

Community Pride

Planting trees together builds social bonds. Neighbors become invested in their neighborhood's future and more likely to engage in other community initiatives.

Frequently Asked Questions

What species of trees do you plant?

We work with local experts to select species that thrive in your region's climate and soil. Popular choices include maples, oaks, birches, and fruit trees. The final selection is made with community input.

How much do neighborhoods need to contribute?

We handle the initial selection and care process. Customer typically contribute through feedback and long-term engagement with our services.

What if I'm interested but my neighborhood isn't organized yet?

That's okay! We can help. Reach out, and we'll work with you to identify other interested neighbors and organize initial meetings. Community building is part of our process.

How long do trees typically live?

Most trees we plant have lifespans of 50–150+ years, depending on species and care. Proper watering, pruning, and stewardship significantly extend their lives and health.

Do you operate in my area?

We're expanding across 8 provinces and growing. If you're not currently in one of our active regions, contact us anyway—your interest helps us prioritize future expansions.
